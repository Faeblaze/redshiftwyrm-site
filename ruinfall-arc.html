Ruinfall Arc

Where the road broke, the flock descended.

Rhagnvyr sends Reilana Sirocclume and her flock to investigate a ruined eastern settlement where Ogre Siege-Breakers have collapsed local order into mud, panic, broken roads, and siege damage.

This is Reilana’s first major command trial under RedShift doctrine: not a court maneuver, not a spy mission, but battlefield authority against brute mass and anti-air violence.

Arc Premise

Ruins Made by Siege

The ruined settlement is not random destruction. It is a warning sign that the eastern campaign cannot grow without control over roads, villages, fort approaches, and local fear.

Why Rhagnvyr Sends Reilana

Rhagnvyr does not send Reilana because the ogres are worthy of his direct attention. He sends her because leadership must be proven through action. If she is to hold sky-routes, command flocks, and represent RedShift authority in the east, she must demonstrate that her wings can answer chaos below.

The ruined village becomes a public test: can Reilana stabilize terror, punish siege-brutes, and prove that RedShift doctrine is not only divine power, but regional order?

What the Ogres Represent

Ogre Siege-Breakers are not elegant rivals. They are ugly battlefield pressure: thrown stone, warped spines, boulder weapons, anti-air force, mud, scars, and brute intimidation.

Their purpose is to challenge harpy superiority by making the air unsafe and the ground catastrophic.

Command Trial

Reilana’s Flock Descends

Reilana’s strength is not just personal flight. It is command presence. This arc tests whether her flock can respond to chaos without losing formation, authority, or purpose.

Investigation

The flock enters a damaged region to determine what caused the settlement collapse, why the roads are broken, and whether the enemy is organized or instinctive.

Anti-Air Threat

Siege-Breakers force harpies to respect the ground. Thrown stone, massive reach, and brute timing turn flight into a risk instead of a guarantee.

RedShift Proof

Reilana’s victory proves that RedShift authority can punish chaos, stabilize territory, and turn fear into visible hierarchy.

Ogre Siege-Breakers

Mass Against Wings

Ogre Siege-Breakers exist as battlefield pressure made flesh. Their bodies are muddy, asymmetrical, scarred, and brutally powerful. They are not designed to seduce the eye. They are designed to break formations.

Against Reilana, they serve a clear purpose: force the harpies to prove that aerial superiority still matters when the enemy can turn the sky into a target zone.
